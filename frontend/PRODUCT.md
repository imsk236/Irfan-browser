# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single manuscript researcher working alone, cataloging ~400 Omani manuscript volumes. Deep domain expertise; comfortable with Arabic scholarly terminology. Works at a desktop, often cross-referencing physical manuscripts. The interface is a scholarly instrument, not a consumer app.

## Product Purpose

A desktop archiving tool (Electron) for cataloging Omani manuscript volumes, recording their physical properties, works they contain, annotations inscribed on them, and tracing scholars who appear across the collection. Success looks like: every volume cataloged, every scholar traceable, every evidence link documented.

## Brand Personality

Scholarly, precise, culturally authentic. The tool should feel like a well-made reference instrument — not sterile, not decorative. Arabic is the primary language; the interface must feel native to Arabic scholarship, not translated from English conventions.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (Notion, Airtable, Monday.com clones) — too soft, too consumer
- Library-catalog UIs from the 1990s — too utilitarian, no visual hierarchy
- English-first tools with bolt-on RTL — layout and terminology must feel Arabic-native
- Heavy dark-mode "developer tool" aesthetics — this is daylight scholarly work

## Design Principles

1. **Instrument, not app** — every element earns its place by serving the cataloging task; no decorative chrome
2. **Evidence first** — uncertainty and confidence are first-class concepts; the UI should make evidence linkage visible at a glance
3. **Arabic-native** — RTL is structural, not cosmetic; Arabic terminology drives labels, not translations of English terms
4. **Density with clarity** — a researcher needs a lot of data in view; density is a virtue when hierarchy is maintained
5. **One surface, zero confusion** — consistent vocabulary screen to screen; no re-learning across screens

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum for text contrast
- Reduced motion support required (researcher may work long sessions)
- RTL direction must be structural throughout, not toggled
- Arabic numerals for Arabic content; Western numerals only for system-level serials (document numbers, IDs)
